# Governance, maintenance & disclosures

This document states how ESGMap is maintained and the disclosures expected of a
country-ranking dataset, per open-science and dataset-publication norms.

## Maintenance model

- **Headline numeric data** (renewable, carbon, CO₂/capita, PM2.5, forest, electricity
  use, mix, history) is regenerated by `scripts/build-data.mjs` from the cited open
  feeds. The GitHub Actions deploy refreshes it on each push and stamps a content hash +
  edition version, so any edition is verifiable and reproducible.
- **Near-real-time overlay** refreshes hourly via the same workflow (`scripts/build-live.mjs`).
- **Curated policy layer** (`scripts/country-meta.json`: Paris/NDC/net-zero, IFRS S1/S2,
  EV share, climate-risk, region, capital, tier) is updated manually from the cited
  authorities; it is slow-moving and dated.
- **Update cadence:** numeric data is effectively continuous (each deploy); the curated
  layer is reviewed on an as-needed basis when authorities publish changes.
- **Issues / corrections:** open a GitHub issue. Data corrections are made in the curated
  layer or by adjusting the ingestion mapping, and are recorded in the build's
  `changelog.json` (added/removed territories and the largest value deltas per edition).

## Editorial choices that affect rankings

These are judgement calls a user should know about before citing a ranking:

- **Country universe & tiers.** ESGMap covers a curated set of ~94 territories. "Rich" tier
  countries carry the full policy/disclosure detail; "base" tier countries show headline
  energy/environment metrics only. Tiering reflects data availability, not importance.
- **Composite weights.** The score uses a transparent but inherently subjective weighting
  (clean power 30% · grid carbon 25% · CO₂/capita 20% · disclosure 15% · climate risk 10%).
  This is **one defensible weighting, not the weighting** — the in-app **Score Lab** lets
  anyone re-weight and see the ranking move.
- **Renormalisation.** Where a sub-score is missing (e.g. base-tier countries have no
  disclosure sub-score) the weights renormalise over what remains. This can let a country
  score well on the components it has while contributing nothing on governance — a known
  structural property surfaced as "scored on N/5 indicators" and in the Limitations.
- **Normative framing.** A composite that ranks nations on "sustainability" encodes values.
  A low score is not a verdict on a country or its people; inputs carry structural bias
  (e.g. disclosure data favours wealthier jurisdictions). See the in-app **Responsible use**
  note and Limitations.

## Conflict of interest & funding

- ESGMap is an **independent, non-commercial** open-source project. It is **not affiliated
  with, funded by, or endorsed by** any of the data providers (Our World in Data, the World
  Bank, Ember, the Energy Institute, IEA, UNFCCC, IFRS Foundation, ND-GAIN, Yale EPI), any
  government, rating agency, or commercial ESG vendor.
- The maintainers declare **no competing financial interests** in any country's ranking.
- ESGMap produces **no financial, investment, or policy advice.** Decisions should rely on
  the primary sources, which are cited with retrieval dates throughout.

## Provenance & reproducibility

Every edition ships a `public/build-manifest.json` (version, content hash, git SHA, node
version, source list + retrieval dates, score weights) and a `CITATION.cff`. The dataset and
codebook are downloadable in the app's **Methodology → Download data** section. See
[`DATA_SOURCES.md`](DATA_SOURCES.md) for the full provenance and licensing table.
